Feature - Financial Plan

What is the Financial Plan Feature in OnePlan?

OnePlan Financial Plan feature helps organizations effectively manage and allocate their budgets across multiple projects and strategies, ensuring optimal resource utilization and cost-efficiency. They are crucial for making informed financial decisions, mitigating financial risks, and enhancing overall project success.

Why Would You Want to Add It?

Project financial planning is the process of determining project costs and developing a budget. Good financial planning has many benefits, including estimating profit, reducing financial risk, and planning for unexpected costs.

The robust financial capabilities in OnePlan’s Financial Plans enables project budgeting, forecasting, and tracking actuals for capital and expense across all work. With the OnePlan Financial Plan, you can track and monitor expenses as the project progresses, analyze your expected and actual costs, react to changes in the plan's finances as they happen, and determine financial gains.

Example Use Case

An example use case for using Financial Plans in OnePlan might be for a CFO or finance manager overseeing several projects. They could create a financial plan for each project, outlining the expected costs, allocated budget, and revenue forecasts.

For instance, if a company is planning a product launch, the Financial Plan will include the costs of product development, marketing, and production, as well as expected revenue from product sales. Or in a construction project, it can be used to track costs of labor, materials, permits, etc against the budget set for the project.

By regularly updating the Financial Plans with actual costs and revenues as the project progresses, they can keep a close watch on the financial health of each project. OnePlan's financial planning tools can help identify if costs are running over budget or if revenues are lower than expected, enabling timely decision-making and strategic adjustments.
