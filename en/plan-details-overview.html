Plan Details

The Plan Details are designed to be the ultimate hub for plan managers, these dynamic forms capture all essential metadata, directly influencing work plans and reports for better insights and smarter decision-making. But it doesn't stop therePlan Details serve as a centralized repository for everything related to your plan, including vital documentation. With unmatched flexibility and functionality, OnePlan Plan Details empower you to streamline, organize, and elevate your workflow effortlessly. Make every plan count with OnePlan!

Plan Details Overview

This article explains what the Plan Details are, and provides an overview of the buttons and features of an example Plan Details form.

What are Plan Details?

The plan details forms in OnePlan are where plan managers will enter all metadata for a given plan. The metadata entered into the plan details directly impacts rollup values in the work plan and reports. So, the more information provided in the plan details, the better.

Beyond just metadata, the OnePlan Plan Details forms are desiged to be your central repository for all information related to a particular plan, including documentation and more. The Plan Details forms are extrememly flexible and capiable, allowing you to further streamline and centralize your work.

Access the Plan Details

There are a couple ways to get to the plan details for your plan.

From the home page, in the Plans panel, click the quick edit menu next to the desired plan and select Details .

From the My Portfolio area, locate the desired plan. Click the quick edit menu and select Details .

You can also use the Quick Edit menu to open the plan details in a pop-out window.

Get Around the Plan Details

The plan details forms are highly customizable, and each organization will likely have their own twist on this form. Additionally, each plan type has its own form, and each form may be a little different depending on the kind of plan it is.

Plan Contextual Menu

Starting in the top right corner, you see this ellipsis next to the plan name. Click the ellipsis to access the plan contextual menu. Here you can:

  • Shared With: Share the plan with other users. You can Add or remove users from the Shared With form.

  • Comments: Add comments to the current plan.

  • Add Favorite: Add the current plan to your Favorite list. You can access your Favorite plans from the Home page.

  • Copy Plan Hierarchy: Here you can copy the current plans hierarchy over to a new or existing plan. For example, you could copy all the plans from one program to another. You could also copy the program and all plans under it.This creates the plans and copies the plan level field values (it does not currently copy schedules, resource plans, etc.).

  • Switch Plans: Locate and go to a different plan from the Switch Plans pop up.

  • Integrated Tools: If your OnePlan group is integrated with another tool, you may access additional menu options to synch and/or launch the other integrated tools.

Plan Action Menu

Below the plan name, are some icons.

  • Shared With: Share the plan with other users. You can Add or remove users from the Shared With form.

  • Comments: Add comments to the current plan.

  • Add Favorite: Add the current plan to your Favorite list. You can access your Favorite plans from the Home page.

  • Integrated Tools: If your OnePlan group is integrated with another tool, you may access additional menu options to synch and/or launch the other integrated tools. In the example, there is a Microsoft Teams integration.

On the left side of the screen, there is the recalculate button, which, when clicked, recalculates all rollup calculations in the plan on demand.

Planning Modules

To the right, you will see all the different Planning Modules available in this plan.

  • Plan Details are where you enter all metadata for the given plan.

  • Plan Financials is where you can enter and monitor the financials for the given plan.

  • Plan Resource Plan is where you can manage the resources assigned to the given plan.

  • The Work Plan is where you track and monitor the execution of the given plan.

  • Reporting (Status Reports, Insights, Visualizer, Power BI Dashboards): OnePlan offers robust reporting options to give you detailed summaries of the given plan with Status Reports, Insights, Visualizer, and Power BI Reporting dashboards.

Process Steps

Here we have the plan process steps. Again, these are customizable, so this is just an example. As you progress through each step, the plan details form changes to meet the needs of the stage.

For example, let's say I start in the Plan step. The plan details form contains the Project information, Associated Key Results, Project Business Case, Project Schedule, and Project Financials sections. If I move the plan forward to the Manage step, the Plan Details form now includes Project Prioritization, Project Health and Project Trending sections.

Click on the Process History icon to access a record of the plan's progress through the various process step phases.

The process steps can also be enhanced and automated with customization and Microsoft Power Automate.

Types of Fields in the Details Form

  • Plan fields are independent fields from plans. These fields are inputted directly, and are not dependent on other fields. These include fields like Name, Description, and Estimated Start.

  • Summary fields aggregate data from the plan fields below it, such as % Done, Actual Cost, or Remaining Effort.

  • Split fields allow you to split plan costs between parent plans.

  • Trend fields allow you to easily see if key metrics are improving over time.

Edit the Plan Details Form

This article explains how to edit a plan on the Plan Details form. The form will differ, as it is configured per your organization. Also, each plan type may have different details forms.

You may also edit the plan details via the Portfolios page using Quick Edit. How do I view and edit plans from the Portfolios page?

Move the Plan from One Process Step to Another

At the top of the Plan Details form, there may be various Process Steps. The current step is highlighted green. Previous steps are highlighted blue. Future steps are highlighted gray. Click on a step to see the status. To see the Process Step in OnePlan views, use the field called Process Step .

To move a plan from one step to the next:

  • Select the stage/step. The name of the step & status will show. Click Next > .

You may also move a plan back to its previous step, if needed.

Additional Fields Based on Process Step

Different sections of the Plan Details form may be shown or hidden based on the current stage/step.

Review the Process History

Click on the Process History icon next to the process steps to access the process step history for the plan.

Update Field Values

Click into any editable field to make a change. Changes are saved immediately.

If it's a choice field, make a selection from the choices.

If it's a date field, select the date from the calendar.

If it's a user field, begin typing the name of the user desired (must type at least 3 letters), and make your selection.

If the field is multi-lines of text, you may also format the text (bold, size, etc.).

  • You can also expand multi-liine text fields into a full screen experience if you are entering a lot of information.

  • You can print out multi-line test fields to share like a document.

Some plan fields are AI enabled (set up by your OnePlan Administrator). AI enabled fields will have a Sofia icon next to them. To use AI to populate a field:

  1. Click on the Sofia icon .

  2. Then enter a prompt or click the microphone icon and speak a prompt for the AI.

  3. Click Generate . The AI will use the information you provide to generate content and populate the field.

Plan Actions

Perform common plan actions such as sharing a plan, favoring a plan, or adding a comment to a plan using the plan actions toolbar (1), or the plan actions menu (2).

The plan actions toolbar varies by organization and is configured by your OnePlan administrator.

Common plan actions:

  • Shared With: Select the icon in the toolbar or Shared With from the plan actions menu to open the Shared With form. Add or remove users from the plan.

  • Add Favorite: Select the icon in the toolbar or Add Favorite from the plan actions menu to add the current plan to your Favorites. Favorite plans appear in the Favorites tab on your Home screen.

  • Comment: Select the icon in the toolbar or Comments in the plan actions menu to open the Comments form. View previous comments and enter a new comment.

  • Switch Plans: Select the icon in the toolbar or Switch Plans in the plan actions menu to open the Switch Plans window. Locate and select the plan that you would like to go to.

Use OnePlan Stage Gate Workflows for Process Steps

This article explains how to use native OnePlan Stage Gates for the Plan Details Process Steps.

OnePlan now supports the creation of plan-level stage gate workflows, allowing you to set up customized approval rules and control field visibility and editability based on the workflow step. Using these stage gate workfdlows allow you to make plan fields required, read-only, or hidden, and automatically updates to fields, workflow steps, or even trigger plan conversions as dictated by your workflow.

Your OnePlan Administrator needs to configure these stage gate workflows. See Create or Edit Stage Gate Workflows for instructions.

Use the Stage Gate Workflow as a Project Team Member

Once you are ready to move onto the next phase of the process flow, click Next , and select the process step you want to route your project to. The rules and approvals set up by your administrator will kick in at this phase to determine if and when the plan can enter the next phase.

If there are rules configured to determine whether a plan can proceed to the next phase, and your plan does not meet the requirements, failure text will appear and the plan will not be allowed to proceed to the next phase or sent off for approval.

If there is an approval process in place in order to move onto a step (i.e. need approval to move the plan from Initiation to Execution), the next process step will become yellow and in a pending state until approved. While you await approval, you can click on the Details button to view more information about the approval on the plan details form.

If your project is rejected from moving onto the next phase by an approver, you (the plan owner) will receive an email notification regarding the rejection with comments from the approver. The rejection will stop the stage gate workflow and push your plan back to the previous step. You can then update the plan and resubmit for approval.

If there is an approval process in place, once your plan has been submitted for approval, you can keep track of those approvals using the Approval work type. Go to the Work Plan List, then select the Approval Work Type. This log will display all previous and active approval items for the plan.

Once the plan is approved to move forward, or your plan meets all the criteria to move forward to the next phase, the step chevron will turn green and move into an active state.

Use the Stage Gate Workflow as an Approver

As an approver, you will receive email notifications when a new approval item has been sent to you. This notification will include some basic information on the plan. From the email, click View Task to be taken to the task approval form.

The approval form makes it easy to review your approval tasks as it includes important plan information in a clean interface without even needing to log into OnePlan. From here you can review the plan detals, then enter an Approval/Rejection Comment , and click Approve or Reject .

All approval items assigned to you will also appear in your My Work area. From My Work, you can click on the item Name, which will open the approval interface. From here you can review the plan details, then enter an Approval/Rejection Comment , and click Approve or Reject . The item will then be removed from your My Work list.

Note

Comments are required for both Approvals and Rejections.

Approval/Rejection Comments will be added to the approval item's comments.

If you prefer review and approve/reject the plan from the plan itself (and have the permissions to access the Work Plan List), navigate to that plan's Work Plan. Then select the Approval Work Type. This log will display all previous and active approval items for the plan. Open the approval item's Quick Edit form. Until approved or rejected, the Quick Edit form for the approval item will include the approval interface (like the one in My Work). From here you can review the plan details, then enter an Approval/Rejection Comment , and click Approve or Reject .
